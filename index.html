Xiaolu Xiong


My Ph.D. advisors are Professor Joseph Beck and Professor Neil Heffernan. My research at WPI focuses on advancing cognitive science via applying data mining and machine learning technologies on education data sets and developing intelligent tutoring systems that help to improve students' robust learning level and long-term retention performance. By combining these two parts of my work, I have provided highly efficient methods to help students and teachers while maintaining great interpretability. Since 2012, there are over 20,000 students used the products of my work. Some of my achievements are:

1) I discovered a feature, called Mastery Speed, which has great predictive power for modeling retention performance. See this paper.
2) I built an adaptive learning system, called Automatic Reassessment and Relearning System (ARRS), for the ASSISTments platform to help improving students' retention performance. It has been featured in this paper.
3) I built an ETL pipeline to support the research of advanced data mining and machine learning at ASSISTments lab. Check out the code.
4) I created a new performance metric, named O-value, which measures predictive models' stability (work in process). Take a sneak peek here.
